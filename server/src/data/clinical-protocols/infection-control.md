# Infection Control and Antimicrobial Stewardship Protocol

## Overview
Preventing healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) and promoting responsible antibiotic use.

## Standard Precautions
- Hand hygiene: 5 Moments (WHO) with alcohol hand rub or soap and water (C. diff — soap and water mandatory).
- PPE: gloves + apron for body fluid contact; surgical mask for aerosol-generating procedures; FFP3 for airborne pathogens (TB, COVID-19).
- Safe sharps disposal: immediately after use into approved sharps container.
- Environmental cleaning: enhanced terminal clean for isolated patients.

## Isolation Policy
| Pathogen | Room Type | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| MRSA | Single room preferred | Until 3 negative swabs |
| C. difficile | Single room + commode | Until 48 hours diarrhoea-free |
| Norovirus | Cohort or single | Until 48 hours symptom-free |
| TB (smear+) | Negative pressure room | Until smear-negative |
| COVID-19 | Single room/cohort | Per current PHE guidance |

## Antimicrobial Stewardship
### Prescribing Principles
1. Diagnose before treating — obtain appropriate cultures before antibiotics where possible.
2. Use the narrowest spectrum effective antibiotic.
3. Duration: use shortest effective course; avoid open-ended prescriptions.
4. Review at 48–72 hours (IV-to-oral switch, de-escalation, stop if no evidence of infection).

### Antibiotic Authorisation Tiers
- Tier 1 (unrestricted): Amoxicillin, Doxycycline, Trimethoprim, Cefalexin.
- Tier 2 (restricted — consultant review): Piperacillin-tazobactam, Meropenem, Vancomycin, Linezolid.
- Tier 3 (specialist approval only): Colistin, Daptomycin, Caspofungin.

## MRSA Screening
- Screen all elective admissions: nose, groin, axilla swabs at pre-assessment.
- Emergency admissions: screen on admission; decolonise if positive (Mupirocin nasal ointment + Chlorhexidine body wash × 5 days).

## Clostridium difficile Management
- Stop precipitating antibiotics if possible.
- First episode: Vancomycin 125 mg oral QDS × 10 days (preferred over Metronidazole).
- Recurrent: Fidaxomicin 200 mg BD × 10 days or FMT after 3rd recurrence.
- Inform Infection Control team of all C. diff cases.
