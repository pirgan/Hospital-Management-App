# Chest Pain and ACS Management Protocol

## Overview
Assessment and management of acute chest pain including ACS (NSTEMI, STEMI, Unstable Angina).

## Initial Assessment
1. Immediate ECG within 10 minutes of arrival.
2. Continuous cardiac monitoring.
3. IV access, FBC, U&E, troponin (high-sensitivity), BNP, CXR.
4. Repeat troponin at 1 hour (0h/1h algorithm) and 3 hours.

## STEMI (ST-Elevation MI)
- Call cardiology immediately.
- Target door-to-balloon time: ≤90 minutes.
- Primary PCI is preferred reperfusion strategy.
- Dual antiplatelet: Aspirin 300 mg + Ticagrelor 180 mg loading.
- Anticoagulation: Unfractionated heparin 5000 units IV bolus.
- Thrombolysis if PCI not available within 120 minutes.

## NSTEMI/Unstable Angina
### Risk Stratification (GRACE Score)
- Low risk (GRACE <109): early invasive strategy within 72 hours.
- High risk (GRACE ≥140): urgent invasive strategy within 24 hours.
- Very high risk: immediate angiography (cardiogenic shock, refractory ischemia).

### Medical Management
- Aspirin 300 mg loading then 75 mg OD.
- Ticagrelor 180 mg loading then 90 mg BD (preferred over Clopidogrel).
- LMWH: Fondaparinux 2.5 mg SC OD (preferred) or Enoxaparin 1 mg/kg SC BD.
- Beta-blocker: Metoprolol 25–50 mg BD if haemodynamically stable.
- GTN infusion for ongoing ischaemia: 1–10 mg/hour titrated to pain.

## Differential Diagnoses to Exclude
- Pulmonary embolism (D-dimer, CTPA).
- Aortic dissection (CXR widened mediastinum, CT aortogram).
- Pericarditis (diffuse ST elevation, PR depression).

## Discharge
- Dual antiplatelet for 12 months post-ACS.
- High-intensity statin (Atorvastatin 80 mg).
- ACE inhibitor + Beta-blocker + consider Eplerenone.
- Cardiac rehabilitation referral.
