# Medication Prescribing Safety Protocol

## Overview
Safe prescribing practices for all clinical staff with prescribing rights at MediCore General Hospital.

## General Prescribing Principles
- Use generic names (INN) unless a specific branded preparation is clinically necessary.
- Confirm patient identity, allergies, and current medications before prescribing.
- Apply the 5 Rights: Right Patient, Right Drug, Right Dose, Right Route, Right Time.

## Allergy Checking
1. Ask patient about all allergies and adverse drug reactions.
2. Document in allergy field — include drug name, reaction type, severity.
3. Cross-react: document penicillin allergy type (anaphylaxis vs. rash) to determine cephalosporin safety.
4. Use AI Interaction Checker before issuing prescriptions with ≥3 medications.

## High-Risk Medications — Enhanced Checking Required
- Anticoagulants (Warfarin, DOACs, heparins)
- Insulin (double-check dose with nurse; avoid IV insulin without VRIII protocol)
- Opioids (verify pain score; document indication; start laxative prophylactically)
- Chemotherapy (oncology pharmacist sign-off required)
- Electrolyte infusions (IV potassium only in monitored setting; max 10 mmol/hour peripherally)

## Dose Adjustments
- Renal impairment: check eGFR. Refer to BNF/renal dose guidance. Key drugs: metformin, methotrexate, NSAIDs, many antibiotics.
- Hepatic impairment: use Child-Pugh score. Reduce doses of morphine, benzodiazepines, many statins.
- Elderly: start low, go slow. Avoid anticholinergics, long-acting benzodiazepines (Beers Criteria).

## Drug Interaction Prevention
- Major interactions to avoid: Methotrexate + NSAIDs, Warfarin + broad-spectrum antibiotics, SSRIs + tramadol (serotonin syndrome risk), MAOIs + any sympathomimetic.
- Document all interaction checks in the prescription record.

## Controlled Drugs
- CD register must be signed by two staff for administration.
- Unused CDs must be witnessed and documented for disposal.
- Stock discrepancies must be reported to pharmacy within 2 hours.
