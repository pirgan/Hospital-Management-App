# Hypertension Management Protocol

## Overview
This protocol covers the assessment, classification, and management of hypertension in adult inpatients and outpatients at MediCore General Hospital.

## Classification
- Stage 1: SBP 130–139 mmHg or DBP 80–89 mmHg
- Stage 2: SBP ≥140 mmHg or DBP ≥90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: SBP >180 and/or DBP >120 mmHg
- Hypertensive Emergency: Crisis with evidence of end-organ damage

## Initial Assessment
1. Obtain two or more readings on two separate occasions.
2. Assess for secondary causes (renal artery stenosis, primary hyperaldosteronism, pheochromocytoma).
3. Order: U&E, LFTs, fasting glucose, lipid panel, urinalysis, ECG, echo if indicated.

## First-Line Treatment
- Lifestyle modifications: DASH diet, sodium restriction <2g/day, aerobic exercise 150 min/week, weight reduction.
- Pharmacotherapy for Stage 2 or high cardiovascular risk Stage 1:
  - ACE inhibitor (e.g., Ramipril 5 mg OD) OR ARB if ACE-intolerant
  - Calcium channel blocker (e.g., Amlodipine 5 mg OD) as add-on or alternative
  - Thiazide diuretic (e.g., Indapamide 1.5 mg OD) as third agent

## Hypertensive Emergency Management
1. IV Labetalol 20 mg bolus, repeat q10 min up to 300 mg total OR infusion 0.5–2 mg/min.
2. Target: reduce MAP by ≤25% in first hour; reach 160/100 in 2–6 hours.
3. Avoid rapid correction — risk of cerebral hypoperfusion.
4. Admit to ICU/HDU for monitoring.

## Follow-Up
- Review in 4 weeks after initiation.
- Annual review once stable: BP, renal function, electrolytes, cardiovascular risk score.
