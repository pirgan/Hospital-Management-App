# Type 2 Diabetes Management Protocol

## Overview
Evidence-based management of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM) for inpatient and outpatient settings.

## Diagnostic Criteria
- Fasting plasma glucose ≥7.0 mmol/L
- 2-hour plasma glucose ≥11.1 mmol/L on OGTT
- HbA1c ≥48 mmol/mol (6.5%)
- Random glucose ≥11.1 mmol/L with symptoms

## Glycaemic Targets
- General: HbA1c <53 mmol/mol (7%)
- Elderly/frail: HbA1c <64 mmol/mol (8%)
- Inpatient: fasting glucose 6–10 mmol/L

## Pharmacological Management

### Step 1 — Metformin
- Start 500 mg OD with meals; titrate to 1000 mg BD over 4 weeks.
- Hold if eGFR <30. Caution 30–45: max 1g/day.

### Step 2 — Add-on Therapy (if HbA1c not at target)
- SGLT2 inhibitor (e.g., Empagliflozin 10 mg OD) — preferred if CVD or CKD
- GLP-1 agonist (e.g., Semaglutide 0.5 mg weekly SC) — preferred if obesity
- DPP-4 inhibitor (e.g., Sitagliptin 100 mg OD) — if tolerability concerns

### Step 3 — Insulin
- Basal insulin: Insulin Glargine 10 units at bedtime; titrate by 2 units every 3 days targeting fasting glucose 4–7 mmol/L.

## Inpatient Glucose Management
- Variable rate IV insulin infusion (VRIII) for NBM patients or major surgery.
- Target 6–10 mmol/L; avoid hypoglycaemia <4 mmol/L.
- Check glucose hourly on VRIII; 4-hourly once stable.

## Monitoring
- HbA1c every 3 months until target; every 6 months once stable.
- Annual: eGFR, urine ACR, foot exam, retinal screening, blood pressure, lipids.

## Hypoglycaemia Treatment
- Conscious: 15–20g fast-acting carbohydrate (e.g., 150 mL fruit juice) and recheck in 15 minutes.
- Impaired consciousness: IV Dextrose 50 mL of 20% or IM Glucagon 1 mg.
