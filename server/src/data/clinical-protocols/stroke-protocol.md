# Acute Stroke Protocol

## Overview
Time-critical management of ischaemic and haemorrhagic stroke.

## Recognition — FAST
- Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call.

## Immediate Actions (within 15 minutes of arrival)
1. Non-contrast CT head urgently.
2. Blood glucose (treat hypoglycaemia immediately: glucose <4 mmol/L → IV dextrose).
3. 12-lead ECG.
4. FBC, clotting, U&E, group & save.
5. IV access × 2; DO NOT give IV fluids unless haemodynamically compromised.

## Ischaemic Stroke
### Thrombolysis (tPA — Alteplase)
- Eligible if: onset within 4.5 hours, BP <185/110, no contraindications.
- Dose: 0.9 mg/kg (max 90 mg); 10% as bolus, 90% over 60 minutes.
- Contraindications: recent surgery <3 months, prior intracranial haemorrhage, platelets <100, INR >1.7.

### Thrombectomy
- Indicated for large vessel occlusion (ICA, M1/M2 MCA, basilar).
- Window: up to 24 hours from last-known-well if penumbra present on imaging.
- Transfer immediately to Neurointerventional suite or specialist centre.

### Post-thrombolysis Monitoring
- Neurological obs every 15 min × 2 hours; every 30 min × 6 hours; hourly × 16 hours.
- BP target: <180/105 for 24 hours.
- Repeat CT at 24 hours before starting antiplatelet.

## Haemorrhagic Stroke
- Reverse anticoagulation urgently.
- Target SBP <140 mmHg (IV Labetalol or Nicardipine).
- Neurosurgical opinion for cerebellar haemorrhage >3 cm or deteriorating patient.
- Avoid antiplatelet and anticoagulant for at least 4 weeks.

## Stroke Unit Care
- All stroke patients to be admitted to stroke unit.
- Early physiotherapy, speech and language therapy, occupational therapy assessment within 24 hours.
- Swallow screening before oral fluids.
