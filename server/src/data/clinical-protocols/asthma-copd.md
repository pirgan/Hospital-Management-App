# Asthma and COPD Acute Exacerbation Protocol

## Asthma — Acute Severe
### Assessment
- Moderate: PEFR 50–75%, SpO2 >92%, can complete sentences.
- Severe: PEFR 33–50%, SpO2 <92%, unable to complete sentences, RR >25.
- Life-threatening: PEFR <33%, silent chest, cyanosis, bradycardia, confusion.

### Treatment
1. Oxygen: titrate to SpO2 94–98% (not high-flow without monitoring).
2. Salbutamol 5 mg nebulised (driven by O2); repeat every 20 minutes for severe.
3. Ipratropium 0.5 mg nebulised every 4–6 hours.
4. Prednisolone 40–50 mg oral OD for 5 days OR IV Hydrocortisone 100 mg q6h if oral not possible.
5. IV Magnesium Sulphate 2 g over 20 minutes for life-threatening or failing to respond.
6. Consider IV Aminophylline for life-threatening (loading 5 mg/kg over 20 min if not on theophylline).
7. NIV/intubation for failure to improve.

## COPD — Acute Exacerbation
### Definition
Acute worsening of respiratory symptoms beyond normal day-to-day variation requiring change in treatment.

### Assessment
- ABG mandatory for SpO2 <92% or suspected hypercapnia.
- Target SpO2: 88–92% in COPD patients.

### Treatment
1. Controlled oxygen: 24–28% Venturi mask; titrate to SpO2 88–92%.
2. Salbutamol 2.5–5 mg nebulised every 4–6 hours.
3. Ipratropium 0.5 mg nebulised every 6 hours.
4. Prednisolone 30 mg oral OD for 5 days.
5. Antibiotics if purulent sputum: Doxycycline 200 mg day 1 then 100 mg OD × 5 days.
6. NIV (CPAP/BiPAP) for pH <7.35 with pCO2 >6 kPa despite optimised medical therapy.
