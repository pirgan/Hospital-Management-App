# Sepsis Recognition and Management Protocol

## Overview
Early identification and treatment of sepsis using the Surviving Sepsis Campaign guidelines.

## Sepsis Criteria (Sepsis-3)
- Life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by dysregulated host response to infection.
- SOFA score increase ≥2 points from baseline.
- Quick SOFA (qSOFA) screening: RR ≥22/min, GCS <15, SBP ≤100 mmHg (2+ = high risk).

## Septic Shock
- Sepsis + vasopressor requirement to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg AND lactate >2 mmol/L despite adequate fluid resuscitation.

## Hour-1 Bundle
1. Measure lactate. Remeasure if initial >2 mmol/L.
2. Obtain blood cultures (minimum 2 sets) BEFORE antibiotics.
3. Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour of recognition.
4. Begin IV crystalloid 30 mL/kg for hypotension or lactate ≥4 mmol/L.
5. Apply vasopressors if hypotension persists; target MAP ≥65 mmHg.

## Antibiotic Guidance
- Community-acquired: Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 g IV q6h + Gentamicin if severe/immunocompromised.
- Hospital-acquired/VAP: Meropenem 1 g IV q8h ± Vancomycin (if MRSA risk).
- Neutropenic: Meropenem 2 g IV q8h.
- De-escalate within 48–72 hours based on cultures.

## Fluid Resuscitation
- 30 mL/kg crystalloid (0.9% NaCl or Balanced salt solution) bolus.
- Reassess: heart rate, MAP, urine output, skin perfusion, lactate.
- Further 250–500 mL boluses guided by fluid responsiveness (passive leg raise).
- Avoid excessive fluid — target CVP is not a primary endpoint.

## Vasopressors
- Noradrenaline first-line: 0.01–3 mcg/kg/min; target MAP ≥65.
- Add Vasopressin 0.03 units/min if noradrenaline dose >0.25 mcg/kg/min.
- Adrenaline as rescue agent.

## Source Control
- Identify and remove/drain infectious source within 6–12 hours.

## Monitoring
- Hourly urine output (target >0.5 mL/kg/h).
- Lactate clearance ≥10% over 2 hours.
- Daily review of antibiotics and organ support.
